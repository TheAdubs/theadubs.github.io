An Ignatian Prayer of Review

Step One of Five

Gratitude

— ✦ —

Before you begin, take about sixty seconds to sit in stillness.
Set down whatever you were carrying. Let your breath slow.
Place yourself in the presence of God.

Become aware that you are in the presence of God, who loves you completely and knows your day in full.

  • What small, easy-to-miss gifts appeared today?
  • Who showed you kindness, patience, or love?
  • What moment made you feel most alive?
Step Two of Five

Review

Walk back through your day as if watching a film — gently, without judgment. See it as God sees it: with patience and mercy. Let no moment be too ordinary.

  • How did the morning begin? What was midday like?
  • Where did you give your best self? Where did you fall short?
  • What moments surprised you?
Step Three of Five

Feelings

St. Ignatius called these movements consolation and desolation. Notice where your heart was drawn toward God today — and where it pulled away.

  • Where did you feel peace, joy, or love? (consolation)
  • Where did you feel anxious, irritable, or distant from God? (desolation)
  • What emotions are lingering with you right now?
Step Four of Five

Prayer

Choose one moment from your review — one that carries weight, whether of joy or sorrow — and bring it before God. Speak freely. Ask for what you need. Offer what you are holding.

  • Is there something to confess or surrender?
  • Is there something to thank God for more deeply?
  • Is there someone you need to pray for by name?
Step Five of Five

Hope

Turn your eyes forward. Tomorrow is a new gift. Ask God for the grace you will need — not just in general, but for the specific moments, tasks, and people you will face.

  • What do you most need from God tomorrow?
  • Is there a relationship that needs your attention or repair?
  • What one intention will you carry into tomorrow?
✝

May the peace of Christ guard your heart and mind.

Your examen is complete. Rest in the knowledge that you are seen, known, and loved.

Anima Christi

Soul of Christ, sanctify me.
Body of Christ, save me.
Blood of Christ, inebriate me.
Water from the side of Christ, wash me.
Passion of Christ, strengthen me.
O good Jesus, hear me.
Within your wounds, hide me.
Let me never be separated from you.
From the evil one, deliver me.
At the hour of my death, call me.
And bid me come to you,
that with your saints I may praise you
forever and ever. Amen.
