Round by Round
Draft Strategy Guide

6-team, full PPR. 15 rounds. With fewer teams the talent pool runs deeper — which means waiting longer on QB, K, and DST than you would in a 12-team league.

Round-by-Round Blueprint

Golden Rules

📍 Best Player Available (BPA)
Always draft the highest VOR player available, not your positional need. In 6-team leagues, talent is abundant — exploit the value.
⏳ Wait on QB
In a 6-team league, only 6 QBs are starters. Don't reach before round 5. Let Allen and Lamar fall, or grab Mahomes at fair value in rounds 4-6.
🏆 Elite TE = Weekly Edge
The VOR cliff at TE is massive. Kelce in round 3-4 is not a reach — it's a positional advantage that compounds every single week.
🎯 Load Up on RB/WR Early
Rounds 1-3 are sacred. If you finish those rounds without 2 elite RB/WR anchors, you're already behind. The rest of the draft is damage control.
🗑️ K and DST: Always Last
Kickers and defenses are streaming positions. Pick them last. Zero competitive advantage is gained by drafting a K in round 10 vs. round 15.
🔗 Handcuff Your RB1
If you own McCaffrey, Jordan Mason is a must-draft insurance policy. Protecting your #1 asset costs one late-round pick. Do it every time.

Value Over Replacement
VOR Calculator & Baselines

VOR = Projected Points − Baseline (last startable player at that position). The higher the VOR, the more weekly advantage a player gives you over what you'd get by simply picking off the waiver wire.

How to Read VOR

VOR > 100: Elite. Generational weekly advantage. Draft immediately.
VOR 50–100: High value. Lock in a clear starter edge.
VOR 0–50: Solid. Starts on your bench or as a flex option.
VOR < 0: Below replacement. Streaming or handcuff territory only.
ADP Value > 0: Market is undervaluing this player — steal.
ADP Value < 0: Market is overvaluing — don't overpay.
Big ADP Value: Best value picks come from ADP Value of +5 or better.
